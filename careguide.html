Plant Care Guide

1. Watering

Watering needs vary between plant species, but a general rule is to water when the top inch of soil is dry. Avoid overwatering, which can cause root rot. Use pots with drainage holes for best results.

2. Light

Place your plants where they get sufficient natural light. Some like direct sunlight (like succulents), while others thrive in shade (like snake plants). Rotate pots weekly to promote even growth.

3. Soil

Choose soil based on your plant type. Use well-draining mixes for succulents and cacti, while herbs prefer loamy, nutrient-rich soil. Consider using organic compost for an extra nutrient boost.

4. Repotting

Plants outgrow their pots over time. Repot annually or when roots begin to grow out the bottom. Choose a slightly larger pot and refresh the soil to keep your plant thriving.

5. Pest Control

Watch out for pests like aphids, spider mites, and fungus gnats. Use neem oil spray or soap water as a natural remedy. Regularly wiping leaves also helps keep pests away.

6. Gardening Tips

Start with easy-care plants like pothos, spider plant, or peace lilies. Group similar plants together based on light and water needs. Clean leaves occasionally and gently remove dead foliage to encourage healthy growth.

7. Maintenance Routine

Check your plants weekly. Monitor soil moisture, leaf color, and any signs of stress. Trim leggy stems and remove yellowing leaves. Dust leaves with a damp cloth to keep them breathing and absorbing light efficiently.

8. Humidity & Air

Most indoor plants enjoy humidity. Mist leaves lightly, use a humidifier, or group plants together to create a microclimate. Keep plants away from drafts, AC vents, and heaters to avoid sudden temperature changes.

9.Fertilize

Fertilize indoor plants during their active growing season (spring and summer). Use a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer every 4-6 weeks. Avoid fertilizing in the fall and winter, as plants tend to be dormant and don't need extra nutrients.
